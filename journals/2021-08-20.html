energetic aliens

https://stephenmalina.com/post/2021-07-01-energetic-aliens-among-us/how do we categorize and identify variance in cognitive stamina andobservnd energy levels between different individuals?

  1. 'energetic alien' archetype

    • indefinitely sustain focus on cognitive tasks for more than thewell documented 4-6 hours a day
    • described as having an abundance of energy
    • obsessed with their work
    • (perhaps) can function well with \<= 6 hours of sleep

    how can we keep working without our focus taking a hit? most thatwork long hours definitely take a hit to their creativity duringextended circumstances.

  2. george church

    • undergrad in 2 years
    • 100 hour weeks in the lab during grad school
    • forgets to eat if nobody reminds him; no hore than once everycouple of days
    • almost all ideas came when dreaming, asleep, or otherbeakthroughs; cites narcolepsy as an enabler of his creativity

  3. lee hood

    • daylong studying without distraction or interruption - keepdaylong food supply nearby to retain focus!
    • miles and pushups every day before work to maintain competitiveendurance

  4. napoleon

    • micromanaged europe for years!
    • achievementx extended beyond the political - to general,politician, art critic, lawmaker

  5. grothendieck

    • ten year period inventing algebraic geometry
    • twelve hours a day, every day, for twenty years!
    • incredible, expressive analogies drawn to mathematical concepts;even better writing!

    lots of peers doing great things, bet impact overall hasn't beenvery profound; can feel like a hustle up an amorphous mountain ofaccomplishment to even learn to understand achievements, but manybecome prisoners of their era; his greatest strength was to bealone, developing new ideas independent of others.

  6. erdos

    • never kept permanent address
    • no interest in relationships
    • all material posessions in two suitcasesz
    • no idea how to cook
    • eschewed anything but the interesting works
    • no interest in non math conversations
    • coffee, amphetamine, ritalin, antidepressant use and overusethrough life
    • quit amphetamines for a month: "couldnt get anything done - you'veset mathematics back a month"

  7. donald knuth

    • work hard up front to pick up all of the material, doing extrawork, then just coasting rathes than getting stuck on the lowerpart of the learning curve at an inopportune moment
    • picked up anything - no saying no! - instead choosing to workharder and longer, to write more and more
    • 'got lucky' and came up with the solution to an unsolved problemhanging out for two hours - then pumped it into the thesis!

  8. asimov

    • work 7:30 AM to 10PM
    • driven by intense desire to prove that he wasn't lazy (???)
    • inspired by brooklyn candy store hustle of his father without anybreaks; 'never good enough' approach to parenting

  9. balzac

    • influenced dickens, flaubert, henry james
    • 4 books a year; worked insane editing schedule!
    • 50 cups of coffee a day, likely contributing to early death
    • always in debt, bounced around with fake names, rough childhood

  10. danielle steel

    bestselling author alive; fourth best of all time 20+ hours a day ofwriting! though often thought of as 'low brow' work, rimilar toasimov

  11. carmack

    60h/week: his sweet spot for happiness and productivity play a cd,pause cd player when not being productive. measure how much cdprogress to determine how much work he'd done!

  12. summary

    'energetic alien' abilities may be latent, coming out with somelevel of effort and focus; those who require less sleep to beeffective seem to have hyopmania as only other positive association.different people vary in different ways! some take a creative andprolific axis, while others take a route of dedication and isolatinoto get it all done.
